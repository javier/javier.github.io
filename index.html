Javier Ramírez

(also known as supercoco9, formatinternet)

Head of Developer —and Agent— Relations at QuestDB and all around happy person.

As Head of Developer —and Agent— Relations at QuestDB, I help developers make the most of their (fast) data. These days developers build through AI agents, so a database that's awkward for an agent is awkward for the developer directing it.

A growing part of my job is closing that gap: writing skills and MCP servers, making our docs machine-legible (llms.txt, markdown, runnable examples against our public demo instance), doubling down on standard interfaces like SQL, REST, and Parquet, and curating a cookbook of common recipes so an agent has the building blocks before it starts. Most of these changes come from watching real agents struggle, then fixing whatever made them struggle. The goal is simple: give agents everything they need to produce correct, fast results with as few tokens as possible.

I also make sure the core team behind QuestDB listens to absolutely every piece of feedback I get, and I facilitate collaboration in our open source repository. Day to day that still means the full DevRel surface: docs, community, talks, demos, and shipping features (these days, via agents myself).

I love data, big and small, and I enjoy processing it with streaming engines, ML models, SQL, NoSQL, graph, in-memory, and time-series databases. I prefer distributed, scalable, always-on systems.

Before working at QuestDB I spent over 20 years developing software professionally and sharing what I learnt with the community. I've spoken at events in over 25 countries, mentored dozens of start-ups, taught for 6 years at different universities, and trained hundreds of professionals on cloud and data engineering.
